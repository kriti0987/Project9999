Jurassic Coast,Dorset and Devon

England

The Jurassic Coast, stretching along the coastline of Dorset and Devon in England, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its stunning geological formations, fossils, and natural beauty. This 95-mile stretch of coastline showcases cliffs, beaches, and bays that offer a glimpse into 185 million years of Earth's history. Visitors to the Jurassic Coast can explore its rugged landscapes, picturesque villages, and historical landmarks. Some notable sites along the coast include the iconic Durdle Door, a natural limestone arch near Lulworth Cove, and the towering cliffs of Old Harry Rocks. Charmouth Beach is renowned for fossil hunting, while the charming town of Lyme Regis offers quaint streets and a glimpse into its fossil-rich heritage. For those seeking panoramic views, the Jurassic Coast Path offers excellent hiking opportunities with breathtaking vistas at every turn.
